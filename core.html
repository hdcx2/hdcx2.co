Core Values and Way of Life

---------

At the heart of Hawaiian culture is the aloha spirit, a philosophy that emphasizes love, compassion, and respect for others. Aloha is more than a word; it's a way of life that fosters harmony and connection among people.

Family, or ʻohana, is another cornerstone of Hawaiian society. The concept of ʻohana extends beyond blood relations to include close friends and community members, highlighting the importance of support and loyalty.

Kuleana, meaning responsibility, reflects the Hawaiian commitment to caring for the land, community, and future generations. It's about recognizing one's role and taking action to protect and nurture what truly matters.

---------



Core Values

Aloha
Meaning: Aloha is more than just a greeting; it represents a deep sense of love, compassion, and respect. It embodies the spirit of kindness and generosity toward others.
Application: It’s reflected in everyday interactions, where people are encouraged to treat each other with warmth and understanding.

Ohana
Meaning: Ohana means family, but it extends beyond blood relations to include friends and community members. It emphasizes the importance of mutual support and interconnectedness.
Application: This value encourages individuals to support one another and work together for the benefit of the group, fostering a strong sense of belonging and unity.

Kuleana
Meaning: Kuleana refers to responsibility and duty. It signifies an individual’s obligation to care for their family, community, and environment.
Application: Embracing kuleana means taking personal responsibility for one’s actions and contributing positively to the well-being of others and the environment.

Malama
Meaning: Malama means to care for, protect, and nurture. It reflects the commitment to stewardship of the land, sea, and cultural traditions.
Application: This value is evident in practices related to environmental conservation, sustainability, and cultural preservation.

Pono
Meaning: Pono represents righteousness, balance, and harmony. It involves acting with integrity and ensuring that actions are fair and just.
Application: Living pono means making ethical decisions, striving for balance in life, and ensuring that one’s actions contribute positively to the community.

Ho‘okipa
Meaning: Ho‘okipa is the concept of hospitality and welcoming guests with generosity and kindness.
Application: This value is shown through open-heartedness and the tradition of treating visitors with respect and care, reflecting the spirit of aloha.

Kupuna
Meaning: Kupuna refers to elders and ancestors. It acknowledges the wisdom and guidance that older generations provide and emphasizes the importance of honoring and respecting them.
Application: Valuing kupuna involves listening to their teachings, preserving their legacy, and seeking their advice in important matters.

Laulima
Meaning: Laulima means “many hands working together” and highlights the importance of collaboration and collective effort.
Application: It’s about working together to achieve common goals, sharing tasks, and supporting one another in community projects and daily life.

Mana
Meaning: Mana is the spiritual energy or power that resides in people, places, and things. It’s a concept of divine energy that influences one’s actions and connections.
Application: Recognizing and respecting mana involves understanding the spiritual significance of actions and maintaining a balance between physical and spiritual realms.

Hōʻikeʻike
Meaning: Hōʻikeʻike means to demonstrate, show, or reveal. It’s about sharing knowledge and skills with others, fostering a culture of learning and teaching.
Application: This value is reflected in the practice of passing down cultural traditions, knowledge, and practices through generations.
